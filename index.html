About

My research lies at the intersection of information theory, machine learning, and wireless systems, with a focus on privacy-preserving and communication-efficient AI.

I develop mathematically grounded frameworks for learning and inference in networked systems, spanning distributed learning, graph analytics, and edge intelligence. More recently, I have been investigating statistical learning problems in large language models, with an emphasis on designing fine-grained watermarking algorithms that enable post-generation edit detection and strengthen content integrity.

Broadly, my work aims to bridge theory and practice in trustworthy AI by building principled methods for robustness, accountability, privacy, and verifiability in real-world intelligent systems.
