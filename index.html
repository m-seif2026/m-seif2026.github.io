I am a researcher at Princeton University working with Andrea Goldsmith and H. Vincent Poor. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Arizona in 2022. My research lies at the intersection of information theory, machine learning, signal processing, and wireless communications, with emphasis on privacy-preserving systems, graph learning, distributed inference, and trustworthy AI.

About

My work develops mathematically grounded methods for modern communication and learning systems. I am especially interested in settings where theory, privacy, efficiency, and real-world deployment constraints all interact.

Recent projects include differentially private CSI feedback for Wi-Fi beamforming, privacy-preserving sensing, hierarchical spectral clustering under privacy constraints, collaborative inference, and watermarking methods for large language models.
